    What is this blog about?

    This year I embarked on a journey to learn Data Science. The purpose of this blog is to act as a self- learning journal as I explore various Data Science and general Computer Science topics. It is a great time to be an autodidactic learner with virtually instant access to countless resources at our fingertips; so much so that learning about as big of a topic as Data Science can seem daunting. Through my numerous internet searches I have kept an ever-growing list of textbooks and articles to read, MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) to take, projects to work on, and the like (a current list can be found in the Resources section). On the internet, everyone and their great aunts have resources to share and opinions about how to proceed; before even starting the learning process I had changed paths about half a dozen times. This blog will serve as a mobile base camp: a home base to come back to throughout my journey to keep me grounded and frequently synthesize things that I learn while allowing me to change directions and take advantage of the flexibility that self-learning offers.

    Plan of attack

    Having done a fair amount of online research and speaking with actual data science professionals, I feel like I have a rough idea of where I need to go. Here are some of the high-level topics I’d like to explore:
    • Computer Science Fundamentals
    • Web Development
    • Statistics
    • Coding in Python
    • Machine Learning
    • Gathering, Cleaning & Munging Data
    • Visualization
    My first step has been to learn enough web development to build this blog as I believe that developing strong fundamentals in more general computer science topics are an important part of my learnings, if they don't directly benefit my Data Science exploration.

    The learning path will by no means be a linear one. It will involve jumping from topic to topic as I seek to push each of them forward. Part of the plan involves updating the Progress Tracker section every 2 or 3 weeks to come up with a to do list or plan of attack for the next 2 or 3 week period. On the way I will publish blog posts, summarizing key learning points as I progress.

    Mountain

    I have likened the self-learning process to climbing an infinite mountain. Here is A Self-Learner’s Guide to Climbing an Infinite Mountain: - There may be many paths in front of you but hopefully with enough thought and guidance you can keep moving upwards. - The journey may never officially end but hopefully the views get better and the journey a fulfilling one. - Things may get a bit messy but hopefully you’ve brought enough underwear.
